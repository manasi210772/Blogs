## **A Normal Evening… Or So I Thought**

It was a Tuesday evening when I checked my viva schedule for the next day. I had my **Advanced AI (AAI)** viva at 1 PM on Wednesday.
Some of my friends from another batch had their **AI in Finance and Banking (AIFB)** viva at the same time.
Since I was really tired that day, and my viva was anyway in the afternoon, I decided not to set an alarm for the morning.



## **A Calm Start to a Chaotic Day**

I woke up at around 8:45 AM. 
After getting ready, I went out with my mother to buy fish. I still remember it was around 9:30 AM when we were standing in the queue for buying fish.
Once we were done, we came back home the same way.
Back home, I had breakfast while listening to a YouTube podcast. After eating, I took a short walk around the house for about 10 minutes to help with digestion. Then I sat on my bed, still listening to the podcast.
Everything felt calm, normal… routine.



## **The Message That Changed Everything**

At around **10:12 AM**, a classmate posted in our WhatsApp group that AIFB students were supposed to reach the lab **ASAP**.
I was confused.
*Wasn’t their viva at 1 PM too?*
I ignored it at first, assuming there must have been some last-minute change.
But then a thought crossed my mind, *if there was a change, shouldn’t there be an official message?*
So I opened the official group and scrolled up.T
here it was!
A message from the previous night.
And suddenly, a strange feeling hit me.
*What if our timing changed too… and I ignored that as well?*
I scrolled a little more.
And my heart literally skipped a beat.



## **The Realization**

My batch was asked to report at **9:00 AM instead of 1:00 PM**.
And it was already **10:15 AM**.
To make things worse, I was supposed to be the **first person** to give the viva.
I kept re-reading the message, hoping I had misunderstood it.                            
I hadn’t.



## **Panic Mode Activated**

I immediately called one of my friends from the same batch.
She said she was still at home. That gave me a *tiny* bit of relief. She also mentioned that based on a snap someone sent her, it looked like the viva hadn’t started yet.
I wasn’t fully convinced.
So I called another classmate.
Same response — the viva hadn’t started, and no one knew when it would.
Still not satisfied, I called a friend from the AIFB batch who was already in college.
He confirmed:
- He was at college  
- My batch’s viva hadn’t started  
I asked him to check with our AAI subject in-charge.
And finally the answer came.
**The viva would start at 12 noon.**



## **Relief… Finally**

That one line changed everything.
I could finally breathe again.
I quickly got ready and left for college.
Honestly, I had planned to study properly before the viva, but after all this chaos, that plan clearly didn’t work out.



## **The Final Rush**

I reached college at around **11:20 AM**.
While I was still on the ground floor, I checked my phone and saw a message from our subject in-charge asking students to wait outside **Lab 410**.
I rushed to my classroom, dropped my bag, and asked a classmate if the viva had started.
They said, “Two people have already gone.”
My heart sank again.
I thought I had completely missed my turn.



## **Plot Twist**

I walked quickly towards Lab 410…
Only to realize—
The viva hadn’t even started yet.
Those two students were just waiting outside.



## **The Unexpected Bonus**

I actually got around **10–15 minutes** before my viva started.
Just enough time to revise a few things.
And surprisingly…
**My viva went pretty well.**



## **Final Thoughts**

That day taught me one very important lesson:
*Never ignore official messages.*
But more than that, it reminded me how quickly a calm day can turn chaotic and then somehow settle back again.
From a peaceful morning…  
To full panic mode…  
To unexpected relief…
It was truly a rollercoaster.
And definitely a story I’m not forgetting anytime soon
