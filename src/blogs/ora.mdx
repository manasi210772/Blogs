## <u>Introduction</u>

On the evening of **1st May 2026**, I visited **Ora Kitchen & Bar** with a group of friends. We had booked a table for **sharp 9.30 PM**, expecting a smooth start to our dinner. But what followed was a mix of waiting, rushing, great food, and a few letdowns making it quite an unforgettable experience.

<br></br>

## <u>First Impressions</u>

Even though we arrived right on time, we were asked to wait for **around 20 minutes**. The place was *extremely crowded.* People were constantly gathering near the entrance, waiting for their turn.

We initially preferred indoor (AC) seating, but considering the long wait, we settled for the **outdoor seating**.

Honestly, it was quite hot, but the ambiance made up for it:           
• Aesthetic fans placed thoughtfully  
• Sprinklers releasing mist at intervals  
• A subtle fragrance in the air  

It created a surprisingly refreshing outdoor vibe.

<br></br>

## <u>Service Experience</u>

The staff seemed a bit **overwhelmed and anxious** due to the rush. The waiters appeared slightly confused at times, which reflected in how orders were handled.

We started with ordering starters, but:
- We were informed that **Asian starters would take ~30 minutes**
- We were also *strongly encouraged* to place our **main course order immediately**

This felt a bit rushed, but we went along with it.

<br></br>

## <u>What We Ordered</u>

### Soup

Even though soup wasn’t listed on the menu, we asked and they did have it!

- **Chicken Manchow Soup**  
  ⭐ *Rating: 8.5/10*  
  A solid start. Warm, flavorful, and comforting.

<br></br>

### Complimentary Starter

- **Avocado Cream Cheese Sushi**  
  ⭐ *Rating: 6.5/10*  

This was my **first time trying sushi**, and honestly, I wasn’t a big fan.  
However, others at the table enjoyed it.

Maybe I missed out by not trying it with the soy sauce provided, so I’d say this one is more of a *personal preference*.

<br></br>

### <u>Starters</u>

Suddenly, the service pace picked up and food started arriving *too quickly*.

- **Tandoor Chicken Tikka (Cheese Stuffed)**  
  ⭐ *Rating: 10/10*  
  Absolutely delicious!  
  • Rich flavors  
  • Perfect bite-sized pieces  
  • The cheese stuffing made it even better  

- **Korean Fried Chicken**  
  ⭐ *Rating: 8/10*  
  I expected it to be spicy, but it wasn’t.  
  It tasted great overall, but a bit more spice would have made it perfect.

<br></br>

## <u>Main Course</u>

Here’s where things got a bit chaotic.

Before we could finish our soup and starters, the **main course was already on the table**, which caused the food to cool down.

- **OG BBC Chicken Curry (Signature Dish) with Roti**  
  ⭐ *Rating: 5/10*  

This was a bit disappointing.  
Despite being described as a bold, slow-cooked curry, it tasted very similar to **butter chicken**                                                 
• Too creamy  
• Slightly sweet  
• Lacked the expected depth  

<br></br>

## <u>Final Dish</u>

- **Chicken Dum Biryani**  
  ⭐ *Rating: 8.5/10*  

A strong comeback!                          
• Mild yet flavorful  
• Tender chicken  
• Well-balanced spices  

A satisfying end to the meal.

<br></br>

## <u>Quantity & Satisfaction</u>

We were a group of **6 people**, and the portion sizes were quite good.  
We managed to finish everything — no leftovers and were **too full to even think about dessert**.

<br></br>

## <u>Final Thoughts</u>

Ora Kitchen & Bar offers:                          
• A vibrant and aesthetic ambiance  
• Some truly delicious dishes  
• But also inconsistent service during peak hours  

### ⭐ Overall Experience: 7.5/10

It’s a place worth visiting for the food and vibe, but timing your visit (avoiding peak rush) might make the experience much smoother.

<br></br>

## Would I Visit Again?

Yes, but definitely with:                  
• A bit more patience  
• And maybe at a less crowded time
