## <u>Introduction</u>

On **28th April 2026**, I visited **Manas Jatra** in Satara and tried their **Mini Mutton Thali**. Even though it was called “mini,” the experience turned out to be quite filling.

<br></br>

## <u>What the Thali Included</u>

The thali had:                               
• Chicken thick gravy  
• Tambda rassa  
• Pandhra rassa  
• Jwari bhakri  
• Indrayani rice  
• Raita  

I am not a fan of raita, so I gave it to someone accompanying me.

<br></br>

## <u>Taste & Experience</u>
<br></br>

### **Chicken Gravy**

The chicken gravy was very good.  
It had the perfect amount of spices and tasted really satisfying.

<br></br>

### **Jwari Bhakri**

The jwari bhakri was quite big and slightly thick, which made it less enjoyable to eat.  
I paired it with the chicken gravy, but that combination felt very heavy.  

After eating just one bhakri, I was already quite full.

<br></br>

### **Tambda & Pandhra Rassa**

Both of these are made from mutton stock:

• **Tambda Rassa** is red and spicy  
• **Pandhra Rassa** is white, made using sesame seeds, and not spicy  

Pandhra rassa tasted good, but tambda rassa felt more like spicy water.  
By the time I reached this part of the meal, I was already too full to properly enjoy or finish it.

<br></br>

### **Indrayani Rice**

I couldn’t eat the rice because I was already full.  

Indrayani rice is slightly sticky and generally tastes very good with non-veg curries, so I do feel like I missed out on that part.

<br></br>

## <u>Overall Experience</u>

Even though it was a mini thali with limited items, it was quite filling.  
Because I got full early, I couldn’t enjoy everything properly.

<br></br>

## <u>Final Thoughts</u>

The thali had good flavors and variety, but it felt heavy overall.  
To enjoy it better, it might help to pace the meal and plan what to eat first.
